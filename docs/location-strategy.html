📍 Point Location Strategy v2

Passified by default. Aggressive only when needed. Zero waste.

The Golden Rule

Don't poll unless something wants the data.

Life360 runs a timer that fires every 15 seconds regardless of whether anyone is looking, whether you've moved, or whether the app is even visible. Point does the opposite — everything is event-driven. The GPS hardware stays off until a specific trigger demands a fix.

There are only 5 triggers that turn on GPS:

1
You open the map. You want to see yourself → poll for smooth rendering.
2
Accelerometer detects movement. You started walking/driving → poll for relay.
3
Someone views you. Nudge arrives → one-shot fix → respond → sleep.
4
30-minute heartbeat. Confirm you're still where we think you are.
5
Geofence boundary. OS wakes us — you entered or left a place.

Everything else is zero. No polling. No battery. No wake locks. The phone sleeps.

⚡ Activity States

SLEEPING
Haven't moved. Nobody looking. App may be backgrounded or killed.
GPS hardware is OFF. Zero polling. Zero relay. Phone is fully idle.
Only wakes on: nudge (someone views you), accelerometer (you move), 30-min heartbeat, or geofence trigger.
GPS: OFF Relay: OFF Battery: ~0%/hr
IDLE
App is open and map is visible, but you haven't moved.
Show cached position on map. No GPS polling needed — you're where you were.
Transitions to WALKING/DRIVING on accelerometer trigger. Falls back to SLEEPING after 2 min if app backgrounds.
GPS: OFF (cached pos displayed) Relay: OFF Render: 60fps (static marker) Battery: ~0%/hr
ACTIVE
Moving (walking, cycling, or slow driving). App may or may not be open.
GPS polling at 3s. Server relay at 8s (batched). If map is visible, interpolate at 60fps.
Speed determines sub-state: walking (<2 m/s) polls 5s, driving (>5 m/s) polls 2s.
GPS: 2-5s (speed-adaptive) Relay: 5-10s (batched) Render: 60fps interpolated Battery: ~2-4%/hr active
FAST
Driving or high-speed movement (>5 m/s sustained).
Fastest polling for accurate trails. Relay at 5s because you're covering ground quickly and viewers need timely updates.
Large distance filter (10m) is fine because at 60mph you cover 27m/s.
GPS: 2s Relay: 5s Render: 60fps interpolated Dist filter: 10m Battery: ~3-5%/hr
GHOST
Ghost mode active. Total silence.
GPS OFF. Relay OFF. Server drops anything if it somehow leaks. Own cached position still renders on your own map only.
Nobody knows you went dark. No "last seen" update. You vanish.
GPS: OFF Relay: BLOCKED Render: own cached only Battery: 0%

✨ Rendering Pipeline

1
GPS fix arrives → stored as targetPosition. Marker doesn't jump.
2
Every 16ms (60fps) → lerp currentPosition toward targetPosition at 30% per tick. Marker glides.
3
When converged (distance <0.000001°) → snap to target. Wait for next fix.
4
Other people → same pipeline. Their WS pushes become target positions. Same lerp. Same smoothness.
5
Camera follows lerped position using moveCamera (instant, no animation fight). Never raw GPS.
6
Trails → Catmull-Rom spline through last 30 min of fixes. Smooth curves, not jagged lines.

Why Point wins on both battery AND smoothness: Life360 uses the same 15s poll for everything — too slow for smooth rendering, too fast for idle battery. Point decouples: 60fps rendering via interpolation (smooth), event-driven GPS (efficient). You can't beat both with a single fixed timer.
Implementation note: The accelerometer-based wake trigger uses Android's TYPE_SIGNIFICANT_MOTION sensor, which is hardware-accelerated and costs essentially zero battery. It fires once when movement starts, then we take over with GPS. This is the same mechanism Google Maps uses for timeline detection.
